### Semester 3: German Idealism and Its Critics
*Status: Not yet developed*

Core figures confirmed:
- Kant (deeper): First and Second Critiques
- Hegel: Phenomenology of Spirit (selections), Philosophy of Right (selections)
- Marx: 1844 Manuscripts, German Ideology, Capital selections
- Nietzsche: Genealogy of Morals, Beyond Good and Evil, Twilight of the Idols

Questions to address:
- How much Hegel is feasible? Phenomenology is enormous.
- Schopenhauer? Influential on Nietzsche and later continental thought.
- Kierkegaard? Often grouped with existentialists but historically belongs here.

### Semester 4: Phenomenology and Existentialism
*Status: Not yet developed*

Core figures confirmed:
- Husserl: Cartesian Meditations, Crisis of European Sciences
- Heidegger: Being and Time (substantial selections)
- Merleau-Ponty: Phenomenology of Perception (selections)
- Sartre: Being and Nothingness (selections), Existentialism is a Humanism

Questions to address:
- How deep into Heidegger? Being and Time alone could be a semester.
- Later Heidegger? (On the Way to Language, The Question Concerning Technology)
- De Beauvoir? Second Sex as existentialist ethics.
- Camus? Or is he too "literary"?

- Benjamin: Work of Art essay, Theses on History, Arcades Project (selections)

## Thinkers Not Yet Placed

- **Arendt**: Human Condition, Origins of Totalitarianism. Where does she fit? Post-Heidegger but not quite Frankfurt School.
- **Levinas**: Ethics as first philosophy. After Husserl and Heidegger.
- **Ricoeur**: Hermeneutics, narrative. Could fit Semester 4 or later.
- **Gadamer**: Truth and Method. Hermeneutics track.
- **Habermas vs. Foucault/Derrida**: The debate. Maybe Semester 6.
- **Agamben**: Homo Sacer, State of Exception. Contemporary biopolitics.
- **Nancy**: Inoperative Community, Being Singular Plural.
- **Rancière**: Disagreement, The Politics of Aesthetics.

## Texts to Consider

- Hegel, *Science of Logic* (selections): For the truly ambitious
- Schelling: Influence on Heidegger, often skipped

- Wittgenstein: Not continental, but Philosophical Investigations is essential philosophy
- Simone Weil: Gravity and Grace, mystical/political
- Fanon: Black Skin White Masks, Wretched of the Earth (postcolonial, connects to Sartre)
