# OpenGrad

A self-directed, AI-facilitated PhD program in continental philosophy.

## What This Is

OpenGrad is a rigorous, multi-year program of philosophical study modeled on doctoral programs at institutions where continental philosophy is central—the New School, Penn State, Stony Brook, and European universities like Essex and Warwick.

The program is:

- **PhD-level**: Equivalent in depth, rigor, and comprehensiveness to a doctoral program
- **4 years part-time**: 10-15 hours/week alongside full-time work
- **AI-facilitated**: Curriculum designed with AI, writing evaluated by AI, discussion deepened by AI
- **Non-credentialed**: No degree, no certificate—only genuine philosophical formation
- **Continental focus**: Phenomenology, existentialism, critical theory, post-structuralism

## Why Continental Philosophy

Continental philosophy is the tradition that takes seriously the questions most urgent to lived experience: What does it mean to exist? How do social structures shape consciousness? What is the relationship between knowledge and power? How did Enlightenment reason become a tool of domination? What forms of human flourishing remain possible?

This tradition—running from Hegel through phenomenology through the Frankfurt School through French theory—provides the most powerful tools for understanding contemporary conditions.

## The Core Distinction

We are unapologetically AI-facilitated. The curriculum is designed with AI. Progress is tracked by AI. Writing is evaluated by AI. Discussion is structured and deepened by AI.

But we read alone, in silence, without assistance. The encounter with the primary text is unmediated. No summaries. No explanations. No AI-generated interpretations until you have struggled with the text yourself.

AI operates at the level of program, not at the level of reading. It is the architecture, not the crutch.

## Program Structure

### Year 1: Philosophical Foundations
- Semester 1: Ancient Philosophy (Plato, Aristotle)
- Semester 2: Modern Philosophy (Descartes, Spinoza, Hume, Kant)

### Year 2: The Continental Turn
- Semester 3: German Idealism and Its Critics (Kant, Hegel, Marx, Nietzsche)
- Semester 4: Phenomenology and Existentialism (Husserl, Heidegger, Merleau-Ponty, Sartre)

### Year 3: Critical Theory and French Thought
- Semester 5: The Frankfurt School
- Semester 6: Post-Structuralism and Contemporary Theory

### Year 4: Specialization and Integration
- Semester 7: Psychoanalysis and Philosophy
- Semester 8: Independent Research

## Requirements

- **Reading**: Primary texts only. No secondary literature until you've formed your own interpretation.
- **Writing**: Weekly responses, unit essays, semester syntheses, comprehensive exams, final project.
- **Rigor**: Graduate-level standards. Critical evaluation. No participation trophies.
